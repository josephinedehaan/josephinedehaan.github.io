foodporn
Food photography is everywhere - social media especially has seen a rise in users showing off beautifully composed and perfectly balanced images of their meals. The concept of food porn has become ubiquitous: many of us enjoy spending time scrolling through an endless display of mouthwatering dishes. But why are these images created? There seems to be a widespread obsession with food, especially with the way food looks. In this body of work, I attempt to understand how much our eyes, thus consequently the camera, play a role in what we decide to feed ourselves with. In the style Martha Stewart's horrifying Instagram food photography and a penchant for the grotesque, I have taken pictures of meals (in whatever form) found at various street food hotspots in London. This exploration of street food was shot with a cheap, digital point and shoot camera - the only one available to me at the time. Paired with the unnecessary use of flash, what ensued was a series of amateur looking images and the creation of a distorted, revolting world. These images seek to bring out the worst of what is usually depicted as neat and enticing.
