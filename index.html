sterile city
Sterile City was born out of a seemingly insignificant event. Whilst coming out of Canary Wharf underground station, I sat down on a sofa to organise my belongings - moments later I was asked to leave. This event stirred up confusion in me: the exit from the station led me to this high-rise building's lobby, an inevitable route to reach the street, yet I was lacking permission to sit down on a sofa which was placed there. More research led me to discover how Canary Wharf is in fact mostly a privately owned part of London - one where the owners can make up rules to their own discretion. Is the metropolis a private or public space? This is the question I was seeking to answer in this body of work. What I found whilst exploring this question is that London is increasingly being privatised and as a consequence sterilised, turning into a dystopian shopping mall where everything is strangely familiar. In this series of images I attempt to take the viewer for a walk into this strange world, made of shiny surfaces and barriers whilst permeated by an eerie stillness.
